Communication Rules
Communication Standards

All staff communication — whether with members, internally, or in public — must meet these standards at all times.

1 · Basic Communication Standards
Every statement made on behalf of Atomic staff must be accurate and verifiable. If you're unsure, say so honestly and direct the inquiry to the appropriate party.
Do not communicate unconfirmed information as fact under any circumstances.
Passive-aggressive, sarcastic, or condescending language is strictly prohibited in all forms.
Do not make official statements on behalf of Atomic without prior coordination or approval from the appropriate authority.
2 · Communication with Community Members
Use language appropriate to the context. Avoid technical jargon that general members may not understand.
Do not engage in open arguments with members in public channels. If a dispute arises, redirect it to a private channel or escalate accordingly.
While acting in your staff capacity, do not take sides in conflicts between members.
Everything you do in public channels reflects on Atomic as a whole. Conduct yourself accordingly.
3 · Internal Communication Between Staff
Internal information — including decisions, evaluations, and staff discussions — must not be shared outside the team without authorization from the RDOM or Executive Director.
Do not speak negatively about management decisions in public channels or in front of community members.
If you disagree with a decision, raise it through the designated internal channels — not publicly.
Criticism of fellow staff must always be delivered constructively and privately, never in front of others.
4 · Official Communication Chain

All reports, escalations, and formal communication must follow this chain:

Support→ PR Associate→ Head of PR→ RDOM→ Executive Director
Bypassing this chain without a valid reason is prohibited.
Using incorrect channels for official matters may be treated as a procedural violation.
5 · Handling Sensitive Situations
Sensitive matters — such as inter-member conflicts, allegations against staff, or serious complaints — must be escalated immediately and never handled unilaterally.
Do not make any public statement regarding sensitive situations until you have received official guidance from the RDOM or Executive Director.
All communication related to sensitive situations must be documented and reported through proper channels.
Staff Rules
Staff Conduct & Standards

Core rules governing how all staff are expected to behave, remain active, and interact with one another.

1 · Attitude & Conduct
Maintain a professional, mature, and responsible attitude in all situations.
Do not act arrogantly, condescendingly, or treat yourself as superior to community members or fellow staff.
Manage your emotions — responding to any situation aggressively or impulsively is not acceptable.
Do not bring personal problems or interests into the staff environment or the community space.
Uphold Atomic's reputation at all times, including outside the server.
2 · Activity & Attendance
All staff are expected to remain active in accordance with their role's responsibilities.
If you cannot be active, submit a Leave of Absence (LOA) before the period of inactivity begins.
LOA submitted after the fact will not be considered valid, except in emergencies that can be substantiated.
Activity is measured by genuine contribution — not just being present.
Unexplained inactivity may serve as grounds for evaluation and ProDeTe decisions.
3 · Inter-Staff Relations
Treat all fellow staff with respect, regardless of rank or seniority.
Senior staff must not pressure or demean junior staff unprofessionally.
Disagreements must be resolved internally and constructively — never brought into public view.
Forming internal cliques or factions that could divide team solidarity is not permitted.
Officially established decisions must be respected, even if you personally disagree.
Unresolved inter-staff conflicts must be escalated to the RDOM.
4 · Violations & Disciplinary Actions
Minor Violation
May result in a warning
  • Inactivity without notice for a short period
  • Failure to follow established internal procedures
Moderate Violation
May result in demotion or a staff strike
  • Unprofessional conduct toward staff or community members
  • Taking internal conflicts into the public sphere
  • Extended unexplained inactivity
Serious Violation
May result in termination
  • Abuse of position for personal or group benefit
  • Actions that demonstrably harm the community or Atomic's reputation
  • Dishonesty toward the RDOM or Executive Director during evaluation

Staff Guide

Operational guides for your day-to-day duties as an Atomic staff member.

General notes for all tickets: Maximum one staff per ticket. You may consult internally when needed, but ownership stays with you. Activity is counted from Ask Staff tickets, Priority Support, all public chat interactions, and voice activity. Purchase tickets do not count toward activity, but each successful transaction earns a Rp2,500 bonus.

Always remember: Claim a ticket before handling it. Never close a ticket without the customer's confirmation. If the customer is unresponsive, use the close request. Submit payment before closing the ticket.

Ask Staff Guide
Handling Ask Staff Tickets

The Ask Staff channel is the go-to place for members and buyers with general questions. When a ticket comes in, here's what you do.

1
When you receive an Ask Staff notification, press the Answer button to claim the ticket.
2
A dedicated thread will be created automatically. Begin assisting the member inside that thread.
3
Respond professionally and informatively. Use language the member can easily understand — avoid unnecessary jargon.
4
If the issue cannot be resolved at your level, escalate it to a PR Associate or the appropriate division — do not leave the member without a response.
5
Once the member's question is fully addressed and there are no remaining concerns, close the ticket.

Priority Support Guide
Handling Priority Support Tickets

Priority Support is reserved for buyers experiencing issues with their scripts. These tickets require prompt and attentive handling.

1
When you receive an "Opened Priority Support" notification, press Join Ticket.
2
Select Claim to indicate that you are the one handling this ticket.
3
Identify the buyer's issue and assist them in resolving it. Stay patient and communicate clearly throughout.
4
If the issue is outside your knowledge or scope, consult internally or escalate to the appropriate staff — never leave the buyer without a response or update.
5
Once the issue is resolved and the buyer has no further concerns, close the ticket.

Purchase Ticket Guide
Processing Purchase Tickets

Purchase tickets are opened by members who want to buy a script. Handle each transaction carefully and systematically — accuracy here directly affects trust in Atomic.

1
When you receive an "Opened Purchase Ticket" notification, press Join Ticket, then select Claim.
2
Show the QRIS code using the .q command. Always verify the current price against the #price channel or latest announcements before proceeding.
3
Once the customer sends proof of payment, verify it thoroughly using the standards below before proceeding.
4
If payment is confirmed valid, use Kimmy's Guard to submit the transaction.
5
Wait for the confirmation embed to appear, then guide the customer through using the script until they're confident and self-sufficient.
6
When everything is in order and the customer has no further concerns, close the ticket.

Public Chat Guide
Public Chat Conduct

Public chat activity across all server channels counts toward your overall activity as a staff member. How you show up there matters just as much as how you handle tickets.

1 · General Standards
Maintain professional language in all public channels at all times. You represent Atomic in every message you send.
Stay composed and constructive — avoid conversations that could undermine your credibility as a staff member.
Do not engage in arguments or heated exchanges in public. If tension arises, de-escalate or redirect to a private channel.
You're welcome to be friendly and personable — but always carry yourself in a way that reflects well on the team.
2 · Staff–Member Interaction Boundaries
Keep a healthy professional distance from members you've only known through the server. Avoid becoming overly familiar in public channels.
If a member reaches out via DM and you don't know them well, maintain the same standards you'd apply in a public channel — helpful, clear, and professional.
If you've known a member for a long time and a casual dynamic exists between you, that's fine — but in public channels, professional tone still applies regardless of how well you know someone.
Avoid entering emotionally charged or personal exchanges with members — publicly or via DM — that could compromise your objectivity as staff.
3 · Activity & Presence
Public chat activity is counted toward your activity assessment. Being meaningfully present in the community — not just answering tickets — is part of your role as staff. Show up, engage, and make the server feel alive.
Jobdesk

Roles & Jobdesk

Responsibilities and key duties for every position in the Atomic staff structure.

The ProDeTe (Promote · Demote · Terminate) process applies up to the Community Manager level. Recruitment for RDOM and Executive Director requires full approval from the RDOM, Executive Director, and Owner.
01
Support
Support Representative
The frontline of community service. Support staff are the first point of contact for all members with general or technical questions.
Manage the ticketing system and ensure every ticket is responded to and followed up on in a timely manner.
Assist members through the Ask Staff channel and public chat, professionally and informatively.
Provide Priority Support for buyers requiring more involved assistance.
Escalate issues that cannot be resolved at Support level to a PR Associate or the appropriate division.
Maintain a friendly, professional, and informative tone in every interaction.
02
PR
Public Relations Associate
Carries out the same direct community responsibilities as Support, with the added role of guiding Support members and serving as a communication bridge to the Head of PR.
Fulfill all direct community service responsibilities — ticketing, Ask Staff, Priority Support, and public chat.
Guide and assist Support members in handling tickets or community interactions when needed.
Handle escalated cases and tickets passed up from Support.
Report patterns, recurring issues, or concerns to the Head of Public Relations.
03
HoPR
Head of Public Relations
Oversees the entirety of the PR and Support divisions, ensuring consistent standards and smooth coordination between both teams.
Remain actively involved in direct community service, as divisions below do.
Monitor and evaluate the performance of all PR Associates and Support members.
Establish and maintain service and communication standards across PR and Support.
Handle cases or issues that could not be resolved at the PR Associate level.
Report division status and developments to the RDOM during evaluations, or proactively for urgent matters.
Coordinate with the Community Manager and RDOM to ensure alignment across divisions.
04
CM
Community Manager
Responsible for the health and dynamics of the community as a whole, and serves as the voice of members, buyers, and active participants in front of management.
Remain actively involved in direct community service, as divisions below do.
Continuously monitor the condition, atmosphere, and dynamics of the community.
Serve as the bridge between the community and the staff management team.
Deliver a community-perspective report to the RDOM at each evaluation cycle.
Design and drive programs or initiatives that maintain community engagement and activity.
Ensure the server environment remains positive, constructive, and aligned with community values.
05
RDOM
Recruitment & Division Operations Manager
The operational nerve center of the staff structure. The RDOM manages human resources, oversees daily division operations, and keeps the organization coordinated and healthy.
Remain active in direct community service, though this is not the primary metric for RDOM performance evaluation.
Manage the full staff recruitment process — from identifying needs through to onboarding.
Receive reports from the Head of PR and Community Manager during evaluations, as well as pre-evaluation notes.
Conduct regular staff performance evaluations and make ProDeTe decisions up to Community Manager level in collaboration with relevant divisions.
Resolve internal issues that cannot be handled at a lower staff level.
Maintain clear inter-division communication to prevent miscommunication or role overlap.
Monitor daily activity across all divisions, ensuring responsibilities are consistently carried out.
Report organizational conditions to the Executive Director regularly, or whenever decisions need to be escalated.
06
Executive Director
Executive Director
The highest authority in the staff hierarchy, responsible for strategic decisions and overall oversight of the entire staff organization.
Remain active in direct community service, though this is not the primary metric for Executive Director performance evaluation.
Make strategic decisions that shape the direction and policies of the server.
Oversee all staff divisions comprehensively and ensure the organization runs soundly.
Resolve matters that cannot be handled at the RDOM level or below.
Provide final approval on major decisions — structural changes, new policies, or high-impact ProDeTe actions.
Participate, alongside the RDOM and Owner, in recruitment for RDOM and Executive Director positions.
